# Smart Campus Parking Management System (SCPMS)

## Project Overview

The Smart Campus Parking Management System (SCPMS) is a technological proposal designed to improve the management and utilization of motorcycle parking spaces at the Universidad Distrital Francisco José de Caldas. The project focuses specifically on the motorcycle parking facility located in Basement 3 of the main engineering campus in Bogotá, Colombia.

Currently, the parking area operates without a structured allocation system. Motorcycles are parked using markings originally designed for cars, which leads to spatial disorder, inefficient use of space, and frequent congestion during peak hours. Users arrive without knowing whether parking is available, which causes unnecessary circulation, wasted time, and operational difficulties for both students and security personnel.

The SCPMS proposes a digital reservation and monitoring system integrated into the university's mobile application. Through this system, users can view parking availability in real time, reserve specific spaces for defined time periods, and manage their parking based on their academic schedules.

The objective of the system is to transform the current unstructured parking model into an organized, traceable, and efficient digital service.

---

## Problem Statement

The motorcycle parking facility in Basement 3 suffers from several operational inefficiencies:

- Lack of designated motorcycle parking cells.
- Irregular vehicle distribution inside the parking area.
- No real-time information about space availability.
- Excessive time spent searching for parking.
- Congestion during peak arrival hours.
- Heavy reliance on manual supervision by security staff.

These issues create a gap between the real physical capacity of the parking facility and the perceived availability experienced by users.

---

## Proposed Solution

The Smart Campus Parking Management System introduces a digital platform that allows the university community to manage parking spaces more efficiently. The system is designed as an extension of the existing university mobile application and provides tools for reservation, monitoring, and automated control.

The system replaces the current first-come-first-served model with a structured reservation mechanism that ensures fair access and better rotation of parking spaces.

---

## Main System Components

### Reservation and Availability Module
A digital floor map of Basement 3 displays all numbered parking spaces and their current occupancy status. Users can check available spaces before arriving at the campus.

### Time-Slot Scheduling Engine
Parking reservations are associated with specific time slots aligned with academic schedules. This prevents indefinite occupation of parking spaces and encourages vehicle rotation.

### User Account Integration
The system connects with the university authentication system and registered vehicle plates, ensuring that each reservation is linked to a specific user and motorcycle.

### Security Alert Subsystem
Security personnel receive automatic alerts when a parking space is occupied by an unregistered vehicle or when a user exceeds the allowed parking time.

### External Overflow Advisory Module
During periods of low demand, the system can identify temporarily unused spaces and provide guidance to users who usually rely on informal parking outside the campus.

---

## Scope

### Within Scope

- Digital management of motorcycle parking spaces in Basement 3.
- Real-time parking availability visualization through the university mobile application.
- Reservation of parking spaces using time slots linked to academic schedules.
- Prevention of multiple simultaneous reservations by the same user.
- Automated alerts for security personnel in case of violations.
- Advisory information for managing overflow parking demand.

### Out of Scope

- Construction or expansion of physical parking infrastructure.
- Management of cars, bicycles, or other vehicle categories.
- Legal enforcement of parking regulations on public streets.
- Parking management in other university campuses.
- Payment systems or parking fees.

---

## Stakeholders

The system involves several stakeholders who interact with or are affected by the parking management process.

Students (motorcyclists) are the primary users who need fast and reliable access to parking availability information.

Campus security staff monitor the correct use of parking spaces and respond to system alerts when violations occur.

Parking administration manages the system and ensures that the available capacity is used efficiently.

The campus IT department provides the technological infrastructure and integration with existing university systems.

University management is responsible for improving campus mobility and supporting sustainable infrastructure solutions.

Non-motorized students benefit indirectly through reduced congestion in shared access areas.

---

## Data Collection Methodology

To analyze the current parking situation and validate the need for the SCPMS, several data collection methods were used.

Direct observation was conducted in Basement 3 to study spatial organization, vehicle distribution, and user behavior.

Structured surveys were applied to members of the university community who regularly use the parking facility.

Usage monitoring was performed to understand parking frequency and typical parking duration.

Environmental correlation was analyzed to connect parking demand with academic schedule patterns.

Process documentation was used to formally describe the current workflow of the parking system.

---

## Key Findings

The analysis identified several critical inefficiencies in the current system:

The parking area lacks specific motorcycle demarcation, leading to irregular spatial distribution.

Users must manually search for available spaces, increasing internal circulation and congestion.

Security personnel rely entirely on manual supervision and visual inspection.

Parking spaces can be occupied for indefinite periods without regulation.

Survey results indicate that most users perceive the parking space utilization as inefficient and support the implementation of a digital reservation system.

---

## System Objectives

The Smart Campus Parking Management System aims to achieve the following objectives:

Improve the utilization of the existing parking capacity without requiring physical expansion.

Provide real-time visibility of parking availability through a digital platform.

Reduce search time and congestion within the parking facility.

Implement a reservation system that prioritizes users based on academic schedules.

Provide traceability by linking reservations with user identities and vehicle plates.

Support security personnel through automated alerts and monitoring tools.

---

## Expected Impact

The implementation of the SCPMS is expected to improve the efficiency of motorcycle parking management within the university campus.

The system will reduce congestion, decrease the time spent searching for parking, and provide a structured mechanism for allocating spaces.

By integrating parking reservations with academic schedules and user credentials, the system ensures fair access and efficient use of available resources.

Additionally, the digital architecture allows future integration with IoT sensors for automated occupancy detection, enabling further automation and scalability.
